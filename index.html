Desmond Tutu

Equality and integrity personified

Here is a timeline of Desmond Tutu's life:

  • 1931 - Born Desmond Mpilo Tutu in Klerksdorp, northwest South Africa
  • 1951 - Secured scholarship for teacher training at Pretoria Bantu Normal College
  • 1955 - Marries Nomalizo Leah Shenxane, a friend of Gloria, his sister
  • 1956 - His request to join the Ordinands Guild to become a priest was turned down due to his debts; these were then paid off by the wealthy industrialist Harry Oppenheimer
  • 1960 - Ordained as an Anglican priest by Edward Paget at St Mary's Cathederal
  • 1966 - Becomes first black leader of the (SACC) South African Council of Churches
  • 1980 - The SACC commits itself to supporting civil disobedience against apartheid; the government seizes his passport, subsequently raising his international profile
  • 1983 - He becomes a patron of the new anti-apartheid United Democratic Front (UDF). Tutu angers much of South Africa's press and white minority, predominantly apartheid supporters.
  • 1984 - Having been previously nominated in 1981, 1982 and 1983, Tutu is informed that he has won the 1984 Nobel Peace Prize. He goes on to share the $192,000 prize money with family, SACC staff and a scholarship fund for South Africans in exile
  • 1986 - Over 1,300 people attend his enthronement as the Archbishop of Cape Town
  • 1988 - campaigns for the release of the "Sharpville Six", eventually resulting in the commutation of their death sentences
  • 1990 - For the first time in 35 years, Tutu meets Mandela following his release from prison
  • 1994 - At his farewell ceremony held at St George's Cathedral, Tutu is awarded the Order for Meritorious Service, South Africa's highest honour.
  • 2005 - He adds his voice to the growing dissent over terrorist suspects held at Guantánamo's Camp X-Ray, stating that these detentions without trial were "utterly unacceptable" and comparable to the apartheid-era detentions
  • 2021 - dies at the age of 90 from prostate cancer
